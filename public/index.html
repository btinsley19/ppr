Post-Labor World Development

The Post-Work Accord

Ratified: 2073

Era: 2040–2075

Preceded by: Automation Crisis

Key Figures: Kaya Lin, Noah Reyes

Overview

Post-Labor World Development refers to the global transformation between 2025 and 2075 that led to the rise of post-work societies, emotionally-centered professions, and human-AI cohabitation.

Job Displacement & Inequality (2025 - 2030)

By 2030, over 40% of global jobs had been displaced by AI agents resulting in the New Great Depression (2029). A new tech conglomerate, Syntheso Corp emerged as the dominant controller of AI agents, acquiring multiple platforms and systems. As automation surged, wealth inequality reached record highs.

Protests & Resistance (2035 - 2040)

Discontent erupted. Widespread protests and digital worker unionization began. AI executives faced public backlash and several were targeted in high-profile acts of vandalism and cyber-activism. In 2038, a pivotal speech by activist Janae Torres titled "We Are More Than Code" galvanized global support for humane tech reform.

Following a class action lawsuit in 2031, Syntheso was mandated to allocate a percentage of its cost savings toward public infrastructure and to open a fixed number of "human-only" jobs annually. These included roles such as: Narrative Steward, Memory Keeper, Digital Kinship Coordinator, and Human Experience Architect

Though the Universal Basic Income Act was introduced in 2035, it narrowly failed to pass. Economic tensions grew across the globe.

Legislation & Reform (2040 - 2050)

Governments began implementing trade-style education systems, allowing citizens to pursue paths of creativity, care, and craftsmanship. New human-centered jobs and co-creative AI partnerships flourished. National happiness indices began to rise for the first time in decades, accompanied by a surge in interest in nature, community rituals, and spiritual practices.

In this time period, we saw a drastic increase in the quality of life as people were now able to find work to fill their time. Company OptIn became the main marketplace for finding new jobs.

New technologies like the "AI Companion" created by Holo allowed people to navigate the world in a purposeful and intentionally crafted way.

A series of legistlation resulted in mandates for cities to have AI-free sanctuaries where humans interacted without the presence of technology.

One notable AI-free Sanctuary in California called Kinvale Commons became well known for its employment of solely human workers, prompting spin-off communities around the country for those seeking a distinctly human experience.

In these AI santuaries, humans do jobs that are automated elsewhere, such as bartenderss and cooks.

Universal Basic Income Era (2050 - )

In 2045, after mounting public demand, the Universal Basic Income Act was finally passed. Citizens were guaranteed a baseline income, with the option to work for additional earnings. Many redirected their energy toward hobbies, life-long learning, travel, and care work, creating an era of renewed human purpose and joy.

We saw a rise in the demand for live entertainment and authentic human interaction. Competitive sports became much more common at a young age and they were taken more seriously.

The Robot Combat League (RCA) gained traction initially, but fizzled out as people preferred human sports like the UFC and professional pickleball.

The Meaning Era (2054–)

Happiness indices continued to skyrocket as people found purpose in their daily lives, living a balance of relaxation, productivity, and kinship.

New professions emerged: Mythweavers, Soul Technicians, Emotional DJs. Society shifted from productivity to presence. Children were taught philosophy and empathy as core curriculum subjects. Spiritual communities flourished, and shared experience became a cultural cornerstone.

  • Experience:
    • Life centers on balance, purposeful work, and human connection.
    • Live events and shared rituals are part of daily culture.
  • Organization:
    • Dynamic, purpose-driven teams replace rigid structures.
    • Workspaces shift fluidly between collaboration and reflection.
    • OptIn as a marketplace to apply for jobs.
    • Competitive sports leagues more prevalent than ever before.
  • City / Environment:
    • Cities integrate nature, community, and play.
    • AI-free sanctuaries offer space for deep presence.
  • Economy:
    • Universal Basic Income ensures freedom to explore meaning.
    • Human services thrive in an open opt-in market for extra earnings.
  • Infrastructure:
    • Spaces adapt to emotional and social needs.
    • Homes, transit, and public spaces respond to intention.
    • Dynamic working spaces.
  • Devices and Tools:
    • AI companions support growth and productivity in balance.
    • Tech remains ambient, ethical, and unobtrusive.
  • Policy and Governance:
    • Decisions are co-created by councils and ethical AI.
    • Policies promote well-being, equity, and inner development.

Key Takeaways

  • As AI began to displace human-labor, public frustration turned into actionable protest.
  • Legislation created to ensure there was human work to be done alongside AI automation.
  • Educational reform allowed individuals to pursue their passions freely.
  • New AI tools and tech gave people more direction in living their lives.
  • Universal basic income enabled freedom to live a purposeful and fulfilling life.
  • Spirituality, community, and rest became central to life.
