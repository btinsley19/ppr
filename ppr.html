Post-Labor World Development

The Post-Work Accord

Ratified: 2057

Era: 2040–2075

Preceded by: Automation Crisis

Key Figures: Kaya Lin, Noah Reyes

Overview

Post-Labor World Development refers to the global transformation between 2025 and 2075 that led to the rise of post-work societies, emotionally-centered professions, and human-AI cohabitation. The turning point was the Post-Work Accord in 2057, which ensured equitable life beyond traditional labor.

Job Displacement & Inequality

By 2028, over 40% of global jobs had been displaced by AI agents. See also: New Great Depression (2029). A new tech conglomerate, Syntheso Corp, emerged as the dominant controller of AI agents, acquiring multiple platforms and systems. As automation surged, wealth inequality reached record highs.

Following a class action lawsuit in 2031, Syntheso was mandated to allocate a percentage of its cost savings toward public infrastructure and to open a fixed number of "human-only" jobs annually. These included roles such as: X, Y, Z, A, B, C (to be filled in).

Though the Universal Basic Income Act was introduced in 2035, it narrowly failed to pass. Economic tensions grew across the globe.

Protests & Resistance

Discontent erupted. Widespread protests and digital worker unionization began. AI executives faced public backlash; several were targeted in high-profile acts of vandalism and cyber-activism. In 2038, a pivotal speech by activist Janae Torres titled "We Are More Than Code" galvanized global support for humane tech reform.

Legislation & Education Reform

Governments began implementing trade-style education systems, allowing citizens to pursue paths of creativity, care, and craftsmanship. New human-centered jobs and co-creative AI partnerships flourished. National happiness indices began to rise for the first time in decades, accompanied by a surge in interest in nature, community rituals, and spiritual practices.

Universal Basic Income Era

In 2045, after mounting public demand, the Universal Basic Income Act was finally passed. Citizens were guaranteed a baseline income, with the option to work for additional earnings. Many redirected their energy toward hobbies, life-long learning, travel, and care work, creating an era of renewed human purpose and joy.

The Meaning Era (2058–2075)

New professions emerged: Mythweavers, Soul Technicians, Emotional DJs. Society shifted from productivity to presence. Children were taught philosophy and empathy as core curriculum subjects. Spiritual communities flourished, and shared experience became a cultural cornerstone.

Alternate Futures

Had key legislation failed, humanity might have descended into civil unrest, resource hoarding, and emotional detachment. One critical moment: the near collapse of the Alignment Bill in 2038. Analysts speculate that without the Torres Speech, the world may have veered toward a techno-authoritarian dystopia.

Key Takeaways

  • Early AI governance and safety regulation was essential
  • Care and emotional labor were redefined and valued
  • Universal basic existence income enabled creative freedom
  • Spirituality, community, and rest became central to life
  • Legislative pressure and collective action played a crucial role in positive outcomes
